# Design-Site References for Daily Market Brief Poster

Scraped from design-oriented sites instead of finance publishers. Use these as visual language references for a one-page Telegram market briefing poster, not as assets to copy.

## Dribbble: Finance Infographic Search
- Source: https://dribbble.com/search/finance%20infographic
- Screenshot: `design-market-brief-references/screenshots/01-dribbble-finance-infographic.png`
- Why it matters: Best for scanning many infographic layout treatments quickly: report covers, metric cards, soft illustration, and finance presentation systems.
- Borrow: poster-like thumbnails, large title blocks, fewer but more intentional chart modules.

## Dribbble: Market Dashboard Tag
- Source: https://dribbble.com/tags/market-dashboard
- Screenshot: `design-market-brief-references/screenshots/02-dribbble-market-dashboard.png`
- Why it matters: Good for modern market UI visual language: dark panels, ticker density, market overview pages, and trading dashboards.
- Borrow: compact market modules, dark-mode contrast, chart-card rhythm.

## Refero Styles: Fintech Website Design Examples
- Source: https://styles.refero.design/design-styles/fintech-websites
- Screenshot: `design-market-brief-references/screenshots/10-refero-fintech-styles.png`
- Why it matters: Best curated visual direction board: Mercury, Stripe, Titan, Slash, Ramp, Wise, and other fintech styles.
- Borrow: “financial command center” feel, editorial serif options, cream/black ledger styles, crisp fintech minimalism.

## Strongest Directions
- **Refero + Bear/Talon-style dark command center:** sharp, premium, more market-native.
- **Dribbble finance infographic + Behance analytics card:** cleaner, softer, more approachable for Telegram.
- **Awwwards Bequant + Behance neon:** more dramatic and tech-forward, useful if the brief should feel like a trading terminal poster.
